Privacy Policy

Last updated: 18 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information the Wordnook mobile game (“the App”, “we”, “us”) collects and how it is used. By using the App you agree to this policy.

Summary

Wordnook is a word puzzle game. We do not ask you to create an account and we do not collect your name, email, phone number, contacts, photos, or location. The only personal-ish data involved is an optional display name you choose for the leaderboard and an anonymous ID used to keep your leaderboard entry. Like most free games, we show ads through Google AdMob, which uses a device advertising identifier.

Information we collect

1. Gameplay data (stored on your device). Your puzzle progress, streak, records, and settings are saved locally on your device. This data stays on your device and is not sent to us, except for leaderboard scores (below).

2. Leaderboard data (if you use the leaderboard). When a score is submitted, we store, via Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore):

  • a randomly generated anonymous player ID (not linked to your identity),
  • the display name you optionally enter (you may leave it blank / “Anon”),
  • your score and word count for the puzzle.

We never receive your real name unless you choose to type it as your display name. Please do not enter personal information as your display name.

3. Advertising data (Google AdMob). We use Google AdMob to show ads (rewarded videos and occasional interstitials). AdMob and its partners may collect and process data such as a device advertising identifier, general device information, and ad-interaction data to serve and measure ads. See Google’s policy: policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

How we use information

  • To run the game (save your progress locally).
  • To operate the global leaderboard.
  • To display advertising that keeps the App free.

We do not sell your personal information.

Third-party services

  • Google Firebase (Firestore) — leaderboard storage. firebase.google.com/support/privacy
  • Google AdMob — advertising. support.google.com/admob/answer/6128543

Your choices

  • Leaderboard: Skip entering a name, or don’t submit scores, to stay anonymous.
  • Ads / advertising ID: You can reset or limit your advertising ID in your device settings (Android: Settings → Google → Ads; iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking).
  • Local data: Uninstalling the App removes the data stored on your device.

Children’s privacy

Wordnook is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us and we will remove it.

Data retention

Leaderboard entries are retained while the leaderboard is active. You can request removal of your leaderboard entry by contacting us with your display name.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.
